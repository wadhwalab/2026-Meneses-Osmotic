# Plain-English Captions For Key Results

These captions summarize what to look for in the generated outputs and
code-generated figure panels.

## Bead Motor Speed

The bead assay follows rotation of single bacterial flagellar motors. When
osmotic shock begins, motor speed drops quickly. Because motor speed reports PMF
under these conditions, this is interpreted as a fast decrease in membrane
energy.

## TMRM Fluorescence

TMRM fluorescence decreases after sucrose shock and recovers after the shock is
removed. This supports the interpretation that the motor slowdown reflects
membrane depolarization rather than only mechanical drag on the bead.

## Cell Area

Cell area decreases during shock, showing that cells physically shrink as water
leaves. This provides physical context for the osmotic stress but is not by
itself the PMF measurement.

## Viscosity Controls

Sucrose and sorbitol make the surrounding liquid more viscous, which should slow
bead rotation. The measured immediate motor slowdown is larger than the
viscosity-only prediction, so viscosity alone does not explain the response.

## Adaptation

During sustained osmotic shock, motor speed partially recovers over minutes.
This suggests that cells adapt and restore much of their PMF, even though the
solution remains more viscous than baseline media.
